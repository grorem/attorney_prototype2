Legal Process

1. Introduction

The attorney will listen to the potential client’s desired goal or legal issue. If the attorney determines that there is no legal case, he will provide the reasons. If the attorney determines that there is a legal case, he will then provide some facts that are relevant to the case; and if necessary, he will find further facts that must be obtained or investigated to fully understand the situation.
2. Evaluation

Once the attorney fully understands the client’s issue or desired goal, he will find relevent aspects of the law to apply to the case and to meet the client’s purpose.
3. Discussion

The attorney will disccuss with the client about the possible actions that the client can take, the process that must be followed to meet the client’s goal, and the estimated time and expense to complete the case.
4. Agreement

The attorney and the client will agree on the course of action to take.
5. Follow Up

The attorney will have regular consultations with the client to maintain a constant communication to improve the success of the case.
